# Ad Psychology — Full Reference Guide

## 1. PATTERN INTERRUPT
**What it is:** Breaking the viewer's autopilot scroll behavior in the first 
2–3 seconds by delivering something unexpected.

**Why it works:** The brain filters out ~95% of ads automatically. A pattern 
interrupt forces the reticular activating system (RAS) to pay attention.

**Techniques:**
- Start mid-sentence ("—and that's when everything changed.")
- Open with a surprising visual (unexpected location, prop, or action)
- Say something counterintuitive ("Stop washing your face twice a day.")
- Break the 4th wall ("Wait, don't scroll yet.")

**Mistakes to avoid:** Cheap clickbait that doesn't connect to your product.

---

## 2. CURIOSITY GAP
**What it is:** Creating an information gap between what the viewer knows 
and what they want to know — making it psychologically uncomfortable to 
stop watching.

**Why it works:** Psychologist George Loewenstein's curiosity gap theory: 
humans feel compelled to close open loops.

**Techniques:**
- "Nobody talks about the real reason [X] happens..."
- "There's one thing every [result] has in common. It's not what you think."
- Tease a reveal: "By the end of this I'll show you exactly why..."

---

## 3. PAIN AMPLIFICATION
**What it is:** Making the viewer's problem feel more vivid, personal, 
and urgent before introducing your solution.

**Why it works:** People are 2.5x more motivated by avoiding pain than 
gaining pleasure (Kahneman & Tversky's loss aversion).

**Techniques:**
- Use specific scenarios, not abstract problems
- Future-pace the pain: "Imagine still dealing with this in 6 months..."
- Name the secondary consequences: "It's not just [problem] — it's the 
  [emotional/social/financial impact] that comes with it."

---

## 4. SOCIAL PROOF
**What it is:** Using evidence that others have succeeded with your product 
to reduce perceived risk.

**Why it works:** Cialdini's principle of social proof — humans look to 
others' behavior to inform their own decisions, especially under uncertainty.

**Types (ranked by power):**
1. Specific testimonial with name and result ("Sarah lost 18 lbs in 8 weeks")
2. Volume proof ("47,000 customers")
3. Expert endorsement
4. Media mention
5. Star ratings (weakest but still effective)

---

## 5. IDENTITY ALIGNMENT
**What it is:** Connecting your product to the identity the viewer aspires to, 
not just the result they want.

**Why it works:** People buy to become someone, not just to have something. 
Identity-based purchases have higher LTV and lower return rates.

**Techniques:**
- "People who take [category] seriously trust [product]."
- "This is for the person who's done settling for [inferior alternative]."
- Frame usage as a character trait: "You're the kind of person who..."

---

## 6. CONTRAST PRINCIPLE
**What it is:** Placing two dramatically different options, outcomes, or 
experiences side by side to make the superior one feel more valuable.

**Why it works:** The brain evaluates everything relatively, not absolutely. 
Contrast creates the perception of value through comparison.

**Techniques:**
- Before/after visual or verbal contrast
- "Old way vs. new way" framing
- Price anchoring: show what they're already spending on inferior solutions
- Word pairs: Tired/Energized, Stuck/Free, Frustrated/Confident
