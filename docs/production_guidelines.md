# 🎬 PRODUCTION GUIDELINES — Shoot and Edit Like a Pro

> **You don't need a studio. You need the right setup and the right technique.**

---

## THE MINIMUM VIABLE SETUP

You can produce a high-performing UGC ad with:

| Item | Budget Option | Pro Option |
|------|--------------|------------|
| Camera | Smartphone (iPhone 12+ / Android flagship) | Sony ZV-E10 / Canon M50 |
| Audio | Phone mic (close to mouth) | Rode Wireless GO |
| Lighting | Near a window / natural light | Ring light / Softbox |
| Stabilization | Stable surface / prop | Gorilla tripod / hand grip |
| Editing | CapCut (free) | Adobe Premiere / DaVinci Resolve |
| Captions | CapCut auto-captions | Submagic / Rev |

**The most important factor in UGC quality is NOT equipment — it's lighting and audio.**

---

## PHASE 1: PRE-PRODUCTION

### 30 Minutes Before You Shoot

**Script prep:**
- [ ] Read the script out loud 3x before filming — know it, don't read it
- [ ] Identify your 2–3 "power moments" where delivery must be perfect
- [ ] Practice the hook specifically — this is the most valuable footage

**Location setup:**
- [ ] Background is clean and uncluttered (or intentionally lifestyle-relevant)
- [ ] No copyright branding visible (logos of other brands, flags, art)
- [ ] Lighting source in front of you, not behind (no silhouettes)
- [ ] Phone is in portrait mode (9:16), at eye level or slightly above

**Audio check:**
- [ ] No loud background noise (fans, traffic, TV)
- [ ] Record a 5-second test and listen back with headphones
- [ ] If using wireless mic — test signal before recording

## PHASE 2: FILMING

### Hook Priority Rule

> **Film the hook last (after you've warmed up), and film it 10+ times.**

The hook is 1.5–3 seconds. Getting those 3 seconds right can 3x your ad's performance.

### Multi-Take Strategy

| Shot Type | Takes to Film |
|-----------|--------------|
| Hook variations | 10–15 (worth it) |
| Full script (A-to-Z) | 4–6 |
| Key emotional moments | 5–8 |
| B-roll shots | 2–3 each |
| Product close-ups | 4–5 angles |

**Between takes:** Stay on camera. Don't stop the recording. Slate each take: "Take 3, hook version B" (this makes editing faster).

### The 5 Essential Shots

1. **Hook shot** — The opening. Eye contact, energy, pattern interrupt
2. **Problem shot** — Showing the pain (frustrated face, showing product graveyard)
3. **Discovery/solution shot** — Introducing the product naturally, not salesly
4. **Product close-up** — Clean macro/close-up of the actual product
5. **CTA shot** — Direct to camera, confident, clear instruction

## PHASE 3: EDITING

### Structure and Pacing

**Cut on action.** Make every cut purposeful.

| Platform | Cuts/minute | Energy |
|----------|------------|--------|
| TikTok | 20–40 | Fast |
| Instagram Reels | 15–30 | Medium-fast |
| YouTube Shorts | 10–20 | Medium |
| Facebook Ads | 8–15 | Medium-slow |

### Text Overlay Best Practices

- **Font:** Bold, sans-serif (CapCut's "Trending" fonts work well)
- **Size:** Large enough to read on a phone in 2 seconds  
- **Duration:** Each text overlay should be on screen for 1.5–3 seconds  
- **Placement:** Bottom third (avoids platform UI) or emphasized center
- **Color:** White text with black outline/shadow (highest contrast)
- **Max words per overlay:** 5–7 words

### Audio Mixing

| Layer | Volume Level |
|-------|-------------|
| Voiceover / dialogue | 0dB (reference) |
| Background music | -10 to -15dB |
| Sound effects | -8 to -12dB |

**Lufs target for export:** -14 LUFS (platform standard)

### Caption / Subtitles Settings

- Font: Bold, readable
- Size: 5–7% of frame height
- Style: 1-3 words at a time (word-pop style) outperforms full sentences
- Check: Review every auto-generated caption for accuracy
- Placement: Bottom center, clear of any product or important visual

## PHASE 4: EXPORT SETTINGS

### Universal Export Specs

```
Resolution: 1080 x 1920 (9:16 vertical)
Frame Rate: 30fps (or 60fps for high-motion content)
Format: MP4 (H.264 codec)
Audio: AAC, 320kbps
File Size: Under 500MB (most platforms)
```

### Platform-Specific Export Notes

| Platform | Max Length | Max File Size | Notes |
|----------|-----------|--------------|-------|
| TikTok | 10 min | 72MB | Compress if needed |
| Instagram Reels | 90 seconds | 250MB | |
| YouTube Shorts | 60 seconds | Unlimited | Highest quality possible |
| Facebook Ads | 240 min | 4GB | Use 4:5 or 9:16 |

## COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

❌ **Filming in portrait then cropping landscape footage** — always film in 9:16 from the start  
❌ **Reading from a script off-camera** — eyes leaving lens kills authenticity  
❌ **Too much green in the background** — causes compression artifacts in export  
❌ **Skipping caption review** — auto-captions mis-transcribe 15–30% of words  
❌ **One take for the hook** — the hook is worth 10+ takes every time  
❌ **Adding too many transitions** — simple jump cuts > complex transitions for UGC  
❌ **Ignoring the first frame as a thumbnail** — the first frame is your thumbnail on most platforms
