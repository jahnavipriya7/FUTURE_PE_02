# Hook Breakdown

## Pain Hooks → Emotional Activation
Target the exact frustration the audience feels.
They stop scrolling because they feel seen.
Example: "I spent $400 on serums and my skin looked exactly the same."

## Curiosity Hooks → Retention
Create an open loop the viewer must close.
Promise to reveal something before delivering it.
Example: "Nobody talks about why [common approach] never actually works."

## Story Hooks → Trust Building
Open mid-story to pull viewers into the narrative.
Invite identification with the creator's journey.
Example: "6 months ago I was [before state]. Here's what changed."

## Controversy Hooks → Engagement Boost
Challenge a widely held belief or conventional wisdom.
Sparks comments, shares, and emotional responses.
Example: "Everything you know about [topic] is actually backwards."

## Direct Address → Targeted Attention
Call out the specific person you want watching.
The brain responds automatically to being called by identity.
Example: "If you're a freelancer working 60+ hours a week — stop scrolling."

## Social Proof Hooks → FOMO + Validation
Lead with a number, result, or community reference.
Leverages our tendency to follow the crowd.
Example: "50,000 people switched to this. Here's why."

## Hook Testing Rule
Always test a minimum of 3 hooks before scaling any ad.
Film 10+ takes of each hook — it's the most valuable footage you'll shoot.
Kill hooks with under 20% thumb stop rate after 3 days.
