# 🪝 HOOK BREAKDOWN — Anatomy of a Scroll-Stopping 1.5 Seconds

> **Every hook has hidden architecture. Understand it to engineer them deliberately.**

---

## THE HOOK EQUATION

```
HOOK = Pattern Interrupt + Emotional Trigger + Open Loop
```

All three elements ideally within **the first 3 words**.

---

## WHAT HAPPENS IN 1.5 SECONDS

Understanding the micro-psychology of scrolling:

| Time | What's Happening | Your Job |
|------|-----------------|----------|
| 0.0–0.3s | Eye registers motion/change | Be visually unexpected |
| 0.3–0.8s | Brain pattern-matches: "Do I care?" | Trigger recognition |
| 0.8–1.5s | System 1 gut-check: "Keep watching?" | Create curiosity/emotion |
| 1.5–3.0s | Decision to engage or scroll | Deliver the hook payoff or tease |

The thumb moves in under 400 milliseconds. You must win before the viewer consciously decides anything.

---

## PATTERN INTERRUPT TECHNIQUES

A pattern interrupt is anything that breaks the visual or auditory expectation of the feed.

### Visual Pattern Interrupts
- **Unexpected framing** — extreme close-up, upside-down shot, handheld shaking
- **Contrast** — pure white on a dark feed, or vice versa
- **Movement** — something suddenly enters frame
- **Text-first** — black text on white screen (no creator visible)
- **Before/after split** — dual frames visible immediately

### Audio Pattern Interrupts
- Starting mid-sentence ("...and that's the part no one tells you")
- A sound cue that's incongruent with the visual
- Silence followed by a pop
- An unexpected emotion (laughing, crying, gasping)

### Verbal Pattern Interrupts
- Contradictory statement ("The best skincare routine is no routine")
- Taboo or unexpected word choice
- Direct accusation ("You're doing this wrong")
- A specific number that demands attention ("I spent $4,200 on...")

## THE 10 HOOK FORMULA BREAKDOWN

### Formula 1 — THE PAIN CALL-OUT
**Structure:** State the exact pain as the viewer would say it to a friend  
**Psychology:** Instant recognition → "This is for me"  
**Best for:** Warm audiences, retargeting, niche products  
**Example:** "I was so tired of breaking out every time I was stressed"

### Formula 2 — THE BOLD CLAIM
**Structure:** State a result that sounds almost too good to be true, but is credible  
**Psychology:** Curiosity + aspiration + social proof  
**Best for:** Before/after products, productivity tools, financial products  
**Example:** "I 3x'd my income in 6 months without working more hours"

### Formula 3 — THE CURIOSITY GAP  
**Structure:** Hint at information they don't have  
**Psychology:** Zeigarnik effect — must close the loop  
**Best for:** Educational content, "secret" or "insider knowledge" positioning  
**Example:** "Nobody talks about why [common approach] never actually works"

### Formula 4 — THE POV SETUP
**Structure:** Invite them into a scenario  
**Psychology:** Mirror neurons activate, creates empathy and self-insertion  
**Best for:** Lifestyle brands, relatable situations  
**Example:** "POV: You finally found something that actually fixes your hair"

### Formula 5 — THE DIRECT ADDRESS
**Structure:** Call out the specific person you want watching  
**Psychology:** Selective attention — people respond to being called by identity  
**Best for:** Niche audiences, tight targeting  
**Example:** "If you're a freelancer working 60+ hours a week — stop scrolling"

### Formula 6 — THE MYTH BUST
**Structure:** Challenge a widely held belief  
**Psychology:** Cognitive dissonance creates engagement  
**Best for:** Saturated categories, skeptical audiences  
**Example:** "Everything you've been told about weight loss is backwards"

### Formula 7 — THE SOCIAL PROOF TEASE
**Structure:** Lead with a compelling piece of social proof before revealing the product  
**Psychology:** FOMO + validation  
**Best for:** Products with strong numbers or testimonials  
**Example:** "This $29 serum has 40,000 five-star reviews and no one's talking about it"

### Formula 8 — THE TRANSFORMATION TEASE
**Structure:** Show or describe the 'after' state without explaining how  
**Psychology:** Aspiration + open loop  
**Best for:** Fitness, beauty, education, career  
**Example:** "6 months ago I couldn't afford rent. Here's what changed."

### Formula 9 — THE LOSS AVERSION TRIGGER
**Structure:** Frame inaction as costly  
**Psychology:** Loss aversion (2x more powerful than gain)  
**Best for:** Productivity, financial, health products  
**Example:** "You're losing $200/month and you don't even know it"

### Formula 10 — THE PATTERN INTERRUPT STATEMENT
**Structure:** Say something unexpected, even borderline controversial  
**Psychology:** Surprise → attention → need to understand  
**Best for:** Any niche, especially for cold audiences  
**Example:** "I stopped trying to grow my business and my revenue doubled"

## HOOK TESTING QUICK GUIDE

Test your hooks against these 3 questions:
1. **Would someone forward this to a friend?** (shareability)
2. **Does it create a question the viewer needs answered?** (open loop)
3. **Is it 100% clear within 3 seconds who this is for?** (specificity)

If you can't answer yes to at least 2 of 3 — rewrite the hook.
