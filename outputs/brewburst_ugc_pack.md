## SECTION 5 | PRODUCTION NOTES

### Camera Setup
- Shoot vertical 9:16 — do not crop horizontal footage. Native vertical only.
- Ring light or natural window light facing you. Avoid overhead lighting (harsh shadows).
- iPhone 14+ or equivalent — no need for pro camera. Authentic look is the goal.
- Keep camera at eye level or slightly above. Below eye level = unflattering and less trustworthy.

### Authenticity Tips
- Film in your actual kitchen or home office — not a studio. Real spaces = real trust.
- Wear normal clothes. Overstyled = branded. Under-styled = relatable UGC gold.
- Slight imperfections are good — a stutter, a laugh, a "wait, let me start over" keeps it real.
- Don't memorize the script word-for-word. Know the beats and talk naturally.
- React genuinely when you taste the product. The authentic sip moment is your most powerful shot.

### Recommended B-Roll Shots
- **CLOSE UP**: pouring concentrate from bottle into glass over ice — the slow pour looks satisfying.
- **CLOSE UP**: frothy oat milk swirling into cold brew — Instagram gold, shoot from above.
- **MID SHOT**: you grabbing the bottle from fridge — shows product in real home environment.
- **OVER SHOULDER**: browsing phone while sipping (morning routine feel).
- **SCREEN RECORD**: 4.9 star rating page with reviews scrolling.
- **LIFESTYLE**: walking out door with drink in hand — the "ready for the day" payoff shot.

### Props
- Clear glass tumbler or mason jar (showcases the color of the brew).
- Oat milk carton or visible pour — trendy and relatable.
- Visible phone or laptop nearby — reinforces "busy professional" identity.
- Messy-but-clean kitchen counter — lived in, not staged.

### Audio Notes
- Record voice in a small room or closet — reduces echo dramatically.
- On Reels: layer trending audio low under your voice (25–30% volume).
- Add captions — 85% of social video is watched on mute.
- First 2 seconds: no music intro, start with your voice immediately.
